Sentiment Analysis is a common real-world application of Natural Language Processing (NLP). It helps determine whether a text expresses a positive, negative, or neutral emotion.

Simple examples include product reviews, customer feedback, and social media comments that people use to share opinions.

  • Tokenization: breaking text into words or tokens
  • Stopword Removal: removing common words that add little meaning
  • Stemming / Lemmatization: reducing words to their base forms
  • Feature Extraction: using Bag of Words or TF-IDF to represent the text numerically
  • Classification: predicting positive, negative, or neutral sentiment

Why This Repository Exists

Natural Language Processing (NLP) is the branch of artificial intelligence that helps computers read, understand, and work with human language. In Week 1 of our NLP coursework, we covered the foundational building blocks that every later NLP technique - including today's chatbots and translation tools - is built upon: preprocessing raw text, engineering numeric features, representing words meaningfully, and modelling language probabilistically.

This repository is our attempt to document those Week 1 concepts as a clear, organised, and genuinely useful knowledge artifact - something we (and hopefully other students) can actually return to and learn from, rather than a one-time submission that gets forgotten.
