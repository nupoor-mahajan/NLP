References

Prepared jointly by: Nupoor Mahajan & Hansa Gusaiwal

The following sources were used as references, background reading, and verification while preparing this repository.

  • Course Material - Class lecture notes and slides on Natural Language Processing, Week 1 module, as provided during the course.
  • Jurafsky, D. & Martin, J. H. - Speech and Language Processing (3rd ed. draft). Stanford University. Available at: https://web.stanford.edu/~jurafsky/slp3/
  • spaCy Documentation - spaCy Official Documentation - Industrial-Strength Natural Language Processing in Python. Available at: https://spacy.io/
  • NLTK Documentation - Natural Language Toolkit (NLTK) Official Documentation. Available at: https://www.nltk.org/
  • Mikolov, T. et al. - Efficient Estimation of Word Representations in Vector Space (Word2Vec paper), Google, 2013. Available at: https://arxiv.org/abs/1301.3781
  • Bojanowski, P. et al. - Enriching Word Vectors with Subword Information (FastText paper), Facebook AI Research, 2017. Available at: https://arxiv.org/abs/1607.04606
  • Devlin, J. et al. - BERT: Pre-training of Deep Bidirectional Transformers for Language Understanding, Google AI, 2018. Available at: https://arxiv.org/abs/1810.04805
  • Scikit-learn Documentation - TfidfVectorizer and CountVectorizer documentation, scikit-learn. Available at: https://scikit-learn.org/stable/modules/feature_extraction.html
