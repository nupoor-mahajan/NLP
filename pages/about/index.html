Before You Begin: Foundational Concepts

If any of these terms are unfamiliar, we recommend reading through this section before exploring Section A.

What is NLP?

Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a field of artificial intelligence focused on enabling computers to read, understand, and generate human language - whether that's a WhatsApp message, a product review, or a spoken sentence.

Why can't machines directly understand raw text?

Computers only understand numbers. A sentence like "I loved this phone" means nothing to a machine unless it is first broken down and converted into a structured, numeric form it can calculate with.

What is a token?

A token is a small unit of text - typically a word, sub-word, or punctuation mark - produced by splitting a sentence apart. Tokens are the basic building blocks every later NLP step works with.

What is a corpus?

A corpus (plural: corpora) is simply a large collection of text used for training or analysing NLP models - for example, a folder containing thousands of customer reviews.

What is a vocabulary?

The vocabulary is the complete set of unique words (or tokens) found across a corpus. It forms the basis for building numeric feature vectors like Bag of Words or TF-IDF.

Why must text be converted into numbers?

Machine learning algorithms are mathematical - they work by multiplying, adding, and comparing numbers. Converting words into numeric vectors (through counting, TF-IDF, or embeddings) is what makes it possible to apply these algorithms to language.

What's the difference between raw text, cleaned text, features, and embeddings?

Raw text is the original, unprocessed input. Cleaned text has noise removed and is normalized (lowercased, tokenized). Features are numeric measurements extracted from cleaned text (like word counts or TF-IDF scores). Embeddings are a special, dense type of feature that also captures meaning and similarity between words.
