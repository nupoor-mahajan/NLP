TF vs TF-IDF

Prepared by: Hansa Gusaiwal
Term Frequency (TF) measures how often a word occurs within a single document, normalized by the document's total word count.
TF-IDF multiplies TF by Inverse Document Frequency (IDF), reducing the weight of common words that appear in almost every document while boosting rarer, more distinguishing words.

Comparison Table

DimensionTFTF-IDF
Working MechanismCounts word occurrences within one document only.Combines within-document frequency with corpus-wide rarity.
Computational ComplexityVery low - simple counting.Slightly higher - requires statistics across the whole corpus.
StrengthsSimple, fast, easy to explain.More meaningful; highlights genuinely distinguishing words.
WeaknessesCommon words can dominate scores unfairly.Needs the full corpus in advance to compute IDF.
Suitable ApplicationsQuick single-document keyword frequency checks.Search ranking, document classification, keyword extraction.

When Should You Use Which?

Use plain TF only for very simple, single-document analysis where corpus-wide comparison is not needed. For nearly all real applications - search engines, classification, review analysis - TF-IDF gives noticeably better results because it accounts for how special a word is across the whole dataset.

In plain language In one line: TF tells you how often; TF-IDF tells you how important.
