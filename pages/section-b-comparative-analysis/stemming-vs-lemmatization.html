Stemming vs Lemmatization

Prepared by: Nupoor Mahajan
Stemming is a fast, rule-based technique that chops off word endings using fixed patterns, without checking whether the result is a real word or considering grammar.
Lemmatization reduces a word to its correct dictionary form (lemma) by using vocabulary and grammatical analysis, often combined with part-of-speech tagging.

Comparison Table

DimensionStemmingLemmatization
Working MechanismApplies fixed suffix/prefix-stripping rules (e.g. Porter Stemmer).Uses a dictionary and grammar rules to find the correct base word.
Computational ComplexityLow - very fast, simple string operations.Higher - needs dictionary lookups and often POS tagging.
StrengthsSpeed; good enough for search/retrieval tasks.Accuracy; produces real, readable words.
WeaknessesMay produce non-words (e.g. "studi"); less accurate.Slower; needs more linguistic resources.
Suitable ApplicationsSearch engines, quick keyword matching.Chatbots, question answering, readability-sensitive tasks.

When Should You Use Which?

If your task needs raw speed and only approximate grouping of word forms - like a basic search engine - stemming is enough. If your task needs accurate, human-readable output - like a chatbot or summarizer - lemmatization is the better choice, even though it costs more computation.

In plain language In one line: stemming is fast but rough, lemmatization is slower but correct.
