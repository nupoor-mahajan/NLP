Section B: Comparative Analysis

Prepared jointly by: Nupoor Mahajan & Hansa Gusaiwal

This section places related NLP concepts side-by-side, comparing how they work, their computational cost, strengths, weaknesses, and best-fit applications - helping build a deeper, more practical understanding than reading about each concept alone.

Nupoor Mahajan

Stemming vs Lemmatization

Both reduce words to a base form, but one uses quick chopping rules while the other uses real grammar and dictionaries.

 Hansa Gusaiwal

TF vs TF-IDF

TF measures how often a word appears in one document; TF-IDF also considers how rare that word is across all documents.

 Joint

One-Hot Encoding vs Word Embeddings

One-hot vectors are simple but sparse and meaningless in relation to each other; embeddings are dense and capture real semantic similarity.
