Glossary

Prepared jointly by: Nupoor Mahajan & Hansa Gusaiwal

Quick, beginner-friendly definitions of key NLP terms used throughout this repository.

NLP (Natural Language Processing)
A field of computer science and artificial intelligence focused on enabling computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language.
Corpus
A large, structured collection of text used for training or analysing NLP models. Plural: corpora.
Token
A single unit of text - usually a word, sub-word, or punctuation mark - produced by tokenization.
Vocabulary
The complete set of unique tokens/words present in a corpus, used as the basis for building feature vectors.
Feature
A measurable numeric property extracted from text (e.g. a word count or TF-IDF score) that a machine learning model can learn from.
Sparse Vector
A vector where most values are zero, typically produced by techniques like One-Hot Encoding or Bag of Words.
Dense Vector
A vector where most or all values are non-zero and meaningful, typically produced by embedding techniques like Word2Vec.
Embedding
A dense numeric vector representation of a word or piece of text that captures some notion of meaning or context.
Language Model
A model that estimates the probability of a sequence of words, used for tasks like next-word prediction and text generation.
N-gram
A contiguous sequence of N words (or characters) extracted from text, used to capture short-range context.
TF-IDF
A statistic combining Term Frequency and Inverse Document Frequency to measure how important a word is to a document within a larger corpus.
Stemming
A rule-based process of chopping word endings to reduce a word to an approximate root form.
Lemmatization
The process of reducing a word to its correct dictionary base form using grammar and vocabulary knowledge.
Stop Words
Extremely common words (like "the", "is", "and") that are often removed during preprocessing because they carry little topical meaning.
Bag of Words
A representation of text as an unordered collection of word counts, ignoring grammar and word order.
Contextual Embedding
A word representation that changes depending on the surrounding sentence, unlike static embeddings that assign one fixed vector per word.
Transformer
A neural network architecture that uses self-attention to process entire sequences of text at once, forming the basis of models like BERT and GPT.
