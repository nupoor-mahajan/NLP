Language Models

Transformer-based Representations

Modern architecture (used in BERT, GPT) that processes entire sentences at once using an "attention" mechanism to weigh word relationships.

language modelstransformersstate-of-the-art
Prepared jointly by: Nupoor Mahajan & Hansa Gusaiwal
1 Definition

Transformer-based representations come from the Transformer architecture, which uses a mechanism called "self-attention" to process an entire sequence of words simultaneously, learning how strongly each word relates to every other word in the sentence.

2 Purpose

Earlier neural models like RNNs process text word-by-word, which is slow and struggles with long-range relationships. Transformers were designed to process whole sentences in parallel while still capturing which words matter most to each other, leading to today's most powerful NLP models like BERT and GPT.

3 Working Principle

The self-attention mechanism assigns an "attention weight" between every pair of words in a sentence, indicating how relevant each word is to understanding another. This allows the model to, for instance, connect a pronoun like "it" directly back to the noun it refers to, even if they are far apart in the sentence.

4 Example
Worked example In the sentence "The teacher gave the student a book because she was leaving", attention helps the model figure out that "she" most likely refers to "teacher" based on learned patterns of language use, even though several other words come in between.
5 Advantages
  • Captures long-range relationships far better than RNNs or LSTMs.
  • Can be trained in parallel, making it far faster on modern hardware.
  • Forms the basis of today's best-performing NLP systems, including large language models.
6 Limitations
  • Requires very large datasets and significant computational resources to train from scratch.
  • Attention computation grows expensive as sentence length increases.
  • Complex internal workings make transformers harder to fully interpret.
7 Applications
  • Modern chatbots, virtual assistants, and large language models.
  • State-of-the-art machine translation.
  • Advanced search engines and question-answering systems.
