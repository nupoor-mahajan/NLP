Feature Engineering

Term Frequency (TF)

Measuring how often a term appears within a single document, relative to the document's total word count.

feature engineeringstatisticsbuilding block for TF-IDF
Prepared jointly by: Nupoor Mahajan & Hansa Gusaiwal
1 Definition

Term Frequency (TF) measures how frequently a specific word appears in a document, usually normalized by the total number of words in that document.

2 Purpose

Simply counting raw word occurrences favours longer documents unfairly. Term Frequency normalizes this count so that word importance can be compared fairly across documents of different lengths.

3 Working Principle

TF for a word w in document d is typically calculated as: TF(w, d) = (number of times w appears in d) / (total number of words in d). A higher TF means the word is relatively more prominent in that specific document.

4 Example
Worked example A 100-word product review contains the word "battery" 5 times.
TF("battery") = 5 / 100 = 0.05.
This tells us "battery" makes up 5% of the words in that review.
5 Advantages
  • Simple and intuitive to calculate.
  • Normalizes for document length, allowing fair comparison.
  • Forms the first half of the widely used TF-IDF technique.
6 Limitations
  • Common words (even after stop-word removal) can still dominate TF scores.
  • On its own, TF does not consider how unique or rare a word is across the whole dataset.
7 Applications
  • Keyword extraction from a single document.
  • Building blocks for TF-IDF based search ranking.
  • Quick relevance scoring in information retrieval.
