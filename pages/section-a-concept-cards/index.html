Section A: Concept Cards

Prepared jointly by: Nupoor Mahajan & Hansa Gusaiwal

This section documents 21 core Week 1 NLP concepts, grouped into four categories: Text Preprocessing, Feature Engineering, Language Representation, and Language Models. Each concept has its own dedicated page covering its definition, purpose, working principle, a worked example, advantages, limitations, and applications.

Text Preprocessing

Sentence Segmentation

Splitting a block of raw text into individual sentences before any deeper analysis begins.

Text Preprocessing

Tokenization

Breaking text into smaller pieces called tokens - usually words or sub-words - so a machine can work with them.

Text Preprocessing

Case Normalization

Converting all text to a single letter case, usually lowercase, so the same word is not treated as different words.

Text Preprocessing

Stop-word Removal

Removing extremely common words like "is", "the", and "and" that add little meaning to text analysis.

Text Preprocessing

Stemming

Chopping words down to a rough root form using simple rules, even if the result isn't a real dictionary word.

Text Preprocessing

Lemmatization

Reducing a word to its correct dictionary base form (lemma) using grammar and vocabulary knowledge, unlike stemming.

Text Preprocessing

Noise Removal

Stripping out irrelevant characters like HTML tags, special symbols, and extra whitespace from raw text.

Text Preprocessing

Text Cleaning

The umbrella process that combines normalization, noise removal, and correction steps into one clean-up pipeline.

Feature Engineering

Bag of Words

Representing a document as an unordered collection of word counts, ignoring grammar and word order completely.

Feature Engineering

N-Grams

Grouping consecutive sequences of N words or characters together to capture some local word order.

Feature Engineering

Term Frequency (TF)

Measuring how often a term appears within a single document, relative to the document's total word count.

Feature Engineering

Inverse Document Frequency (IDF)

Measuring how rare or common a term is across an entire collection of documents, to down-weight overly common words.

Feature Engineering

TF-IDF

Combining Term Frequency and Inverse Document Frequency into one score that highlights words that are both frequent locally and rare globally.

Language Representation

One-Hot Encoding

Representing each word as a vector with a single '1' at its position in the vocabulary and '0' everywhere else.

Language Representation

Word Embeddings

Representing words as dense numeric vectors so that similar words end up close together in the vector space.

Language Representation

Word2Vec

A pioneering neural network method (by Google, 2013) that learns word embeddings using two training strategies: CBOW and Skip-gram.

Language Representation

FastText

An embedding method by Facebook AI that represents words as collections of character n-grams, handling rare and unseen words better than Word2Vec.

Language Representation

Contextual Embeddings

Embeddings that change based on surrounding words, so the same word gets a different vector depending on its meaning in context.

Language Models

Statistical Language Models

Models that predict the probability of a word sequence using statistics such as word frequencies and N-gram counts.

Language Models

Neural Language Models

Language models built using neural networks (like RNNs and LSTMs) that learn word probabilities through training rather than raw counting.

Language Models

Transformer-based Representations

Modern architecture (used in BERT, GPT) that processes entire sentences at once using an "attention" mechanism to weigh word relationships.
