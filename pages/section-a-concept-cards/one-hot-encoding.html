One-Hot Encoding

Representing each word as a vector with a single '1' at its position in the vocabulary and '0' everywhere else.

representationvectorizationsparse
Prepared jointly by: Nupoor Mahajan & Hansa Gusaiwal
1 Definition

One-hot encoding represents each word in a vocabulary as a vector filled with zeros, except for a single '1' at the position that uniquely identifies that word.

2 Purpose

Machine learning models need numeric input. One-hot encoding is the most basic way to convert categorical items like words into unique numeric vectors, without implying any order or relationship between them.

3 Working Principle

If the vocabulary has V unique words, each word is represented by a vector of length V. The position corresponding to that particular word is set to 1, and every other position is 0.

4 Example
Worked example Vocabulary: [college, exam, result].
One-hot vectors: "college" → [1,0,0], "exam" → [0,1,0], "result" → [0,0,1].
5 Advantages
  • Extremely simple to understand and implement.
  • Guarantees that every word gets a distinct, unambiguous representation.
6 Limitations
  • Vectors become huge and mostly empty (sparse) as vocabulary size grows.
  • Does not capture any similarity between related words - "good" and "great" are just as different as "good" and "table".
  • Not memory-efficient for large, real-world vocabularies.
7 Applications
  • Small-scale categorical feature encoding in traditional machine learning.
  • Teaching foundational vectorization concepts before moving to embeddings.
  • Simple lookup tables in early NLP pipelines.
