Word2Vec

A pioneering neural network method (by Google, 2013) that learns word embeddings using two training strategies: CBOW and Skip-gram.

representationembeddingslandmark model
Prepared jointly by: Nupoor Mahajan & Hansa Gusaiwal
1 Definition

Word2Vec is a neural network-based technique, introduced by Google researchers in 2013, that learns dense word embeddings by predicting words from their surrounding context (or vice versa).

2 Purpose

Word2Vec was one of the first widely successful methods to produce high-quality word embeddings efficiently, making semantic word similarity computationally practical for everyday NLP tasks.

3 Working Principle

Word2Vec uses one of two architectures: CBOW (Continuous Bag of Words), which predicts a target word from its surrounding context words, or Skip-gram, which predicts surrounding context words given a target word. Training on this prediction task forces the model to learn meaningful vector representations.

4 Example
Worked example Given the sentence "the shopkeeper gave a fair price", CBOW would try to predict "fair" using the surrounding words "gave", "a", "price", while Skip-gram would try to predict those surrounding words given "fair".
5 Advantages
  • Efficient to train even on large text corpora.
  • Produces embeddings that capture meaningful semantic relationships.
  • Pre-trained Word2Vec models are freely available and easy to reuse.
6 Limitations
  • Treats each word as a whole unit, so it cannot handle words it has never seen before (out-of-vocabulary words).
  • Produces one fixed vector per word, ignoring different meanings in different contexts.
7 Applications
  • Recommendation systems based on textual similarity.
  • Feature input for downstream classifiers in sentiment analysis.
  • Finding related search terms or synonyms.
