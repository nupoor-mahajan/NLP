Text Preprocessing

Text Cleaning

The umbrella process that combines normalization, noise removal, and correction steps into one clean-up pipeline.

preprocessingpipeline step
Prepared jointly by: Nupoor Mahajan & Hansa Gusaiwal
1 Definition

Text cleaning refers to the overall set of steps - including case normalization, noise removal, punctuation handling, and spelling correction - applied to raw text to prepare it for reliable NLP processing.

2 Purpose

Raw text collected from any real source is messy. A consistent cleaning pipeline ensures that every document entering the system follows the same format, which is essential for fair comparison and accurate feature extraction.

3 Working Principle

Text cleaning typically chains together multiple smaller steps in sequence: lowercasing, removing punctuation and special characters, removing extra whitespace, correcting common typos, and sometimes expanding contractions ("don't" → "do not") before tokenization happens.

4 Example
Worked example Raw: "I LOVED the movie!!! Its soooo good :) "
After cleaning: "i loved the movie its so good" - ready for tokenization and further feature extraction.
5 Advantages
  • Creates a standardised, high-quality dataset.
  • Improves the reliability of every later NLP step.
  • Reduces vocabulary bloat caused by inconsistent formatting.
6 Limitations
  • A cleaning pipeline built for one domain may not transfer well to another.
  • Excessive cleaning risks losing meaningful signals like emphasis or sentiment cues.
7 Applications
  • Preparing customer review datasets for sentiment analysis (as in an Amazon reviews project).
  • Cleaning survey or feedback text before analysis.
  • Any machine learning pipeline that starts from raw text data.
