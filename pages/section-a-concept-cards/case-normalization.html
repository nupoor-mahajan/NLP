Case Normalization

Converting all text to a single letter case, usually lowercase, so the same word is not treated as different words.

preprocessingtext cleaning
Prepared jointly by: Nupoor Mahajan & Hansa Gusaiwal
1 Definition

Case normalization (also called case folding) is the process of converting all characters in a text to a consistent case, most commonly lowercase, before further processing.

2 Purpose

Without normalization, a computer treats "College", "college", and "COLLEGE" as three completely different tokens, even though they mean the same thing. Normalizing case keeps the vocabulary compact and consistent.

3 Working Principle

Every character in the text is passed through a lowercasing (or uppercasing) function. This is usually one of the simplest steps in a preprocessing pipeline and is applied right after tokenization or even before it.

4 Example
Worked example Input: "NLP is Fun and NLP is Useful."
After normalization: "nlp is fun and nlp is useful."
Now both occurrences of "NLP" are recognised as the same token.
5 Advantages
  • Reduces vocabulary size, which saves memory and computation.
  • Prevents duplicate counting of the same word in different cases.
  • Very fast and simple to implement.
6 Limitations
  • Can remove useful information - for example, "India" (a country) versus "india" (rare lowercase use) or acronyms like "WHO" versus the word "who".
  • Not ideal for tasks like Named Entity Recognition where capitalization is a helpful clue.
7 Applications
  • Search engines matching queries regardless of case.
  • Spam filters and keyword-based classifiers.
  • Preprocessing step before Bag of Words or TF-IDF.
