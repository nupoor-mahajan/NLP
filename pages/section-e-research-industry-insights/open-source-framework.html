Open-Source Framework: spaCy

Prepared jointly by: Nupoor Mahajan & Hansa Gusaiwal

spaCy is a popular open-source Python library built specifically for industrial-strength Natural Language Processing. It is designed to be fast, production-ready, and easy to use for real applications, rather than just academic experimentation.

Why It's Relevant to Week 1 Concepts

spaCy provides ready-made, highly optimised implementations of almost every Week 1 concept covered in this repository - tokenization, sentence segmentation, lemmatization, and even contextual word representations - so developers don't need to build these from scratch.

How It Helps with Preprocessing

  • Tokenization: spaCy's tokenizer correctly handles punctuation, contractions, and special cases out of the box.
  • Lemmatization: Built-in lemmatizers use linguistic rules and vocabulary lookups for accurate base-word conversion.
  • Sentence Segmentation: spaCy reliably splits paragraphs into sentences, even with tricky abbreviations.
  • Named Entity Recognition & POS Tagging: spaCy goes beyond Week 1 basics, identifying names, places, and grammatical roles automatically.
A note on NLTK NLTK (Natural Language Toolkit) is another widely used, beginner-friendly Python library, often preferred in academic and teaching settings because it exposes the underlying logic of each step more transparently. spaCy, in contrast, is optimised for speed and production use. Many students - including us - use NLTK to learn a concept and spaCy when building something more polished.
