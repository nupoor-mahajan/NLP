Summary: Foundations, Research, and Industry

Prepared jointly by: Nupoor Mahajan & Hansa Gusaiwal

The Week 1 foundations covered in this repository - tokenization, normalization, feature engineering, and language representation - are not just academic exercises. They are the same building blocks used in cutting-edge research (like instruction-tuned language models) and everyday industry systems (like customer support chatbots and review analysis tools).

What has changed over time is scale and sophistication - from simple rule-based stemmers to transformer-based contextual embeddings - but the underlying questions remain the same: how do we turn messy human language into something a machine can reliably work with, and how do we do that fairly across many different languages and communities?

This is exactly why these basics still matter: every advanced NLP system, no matter how impressive, still begins with clean tokens and a well-chosen representation of words.
